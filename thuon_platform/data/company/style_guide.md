# Proposal Style Guide

## Tone of Voice
- **Primary tone:** [e.g., confident, collaborative, technically authoritative]
- **Avoid:** [e.g., passive voice, hedge words like "try", "hope", "may"]
- **Always:** [e.g., lead with the client benefit, use active future tense "We will..."]

## Formatting Rules
- **Font:** [Times New Roman 12pt / Arial 11pt — match RFP instructions]
- **Margins:** [1 inch unless specified otherwise]
- **Headers:** Bold, numbered per RFP section structure
- **Page limits:** Strictly observed — never exceed
- **Figure/table captions:** State the benefit, not just a title (e.g., "Figure 1: Our CI/CD Pipeline Cuts Deployment Risk by 60%")

## Writing Rules
- **Sentence length:** Max 25 words per sentence in technical sections
- **Paragraph length:** Max 5 sentences
- **Bullet lists:** Use for 3+ items; capitalize first word; no period if fragment
- **Numbers:** Spell out one through nine; use numerals for 10+; always include units
- **Acronyms:** Define on first use, then use acronym consistently
- **Percentages:** Always use "%" not "percent" in tables; "percent" in body text

## Forbidden Phrases
- "We are pleased to submit..."
- "Our company has extensive experience..."
- "We understand the importance of..."
- "We look forward to the opportunity..."
- Any phrase that could apply to any company

## Company Name Usage
- First mention in a section: full legal name
- Subsequent: [Short name or "we" / "our team"]

## Proposal-Specific Vocabulary
- Use client's own terminology from the RFP (mirror their language)
- "Client" → use their actual agency/company name
- "Solution" → match their preferred term (solution / system / platform / approach)
